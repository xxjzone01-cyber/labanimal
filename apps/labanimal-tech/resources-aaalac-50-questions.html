AAALAC 50 Essential Questions

A complete checklist to help your laboratory animal facility prepare for AAALAC accreditation review. Covers facility management, animal welfare, IACUC procedures, and more.

Preview: First 5 Questions

50 questions total, covering all key areas of AAALAC accreditation

1

Does the facility have a written Animal Care and Use Program (ACUP)?

The ACUP is a foundational document for AAALAC accreditation. It should cover animal care standards, use procedures, personnel training requirements, and emergency plans. Ensure the plan is regularly reviewed and updated, and approved by institutional leadership.

IACUC Procedures Basic Requirement
2

Does the IACUC committee composition meet requirements?

AAALAC requires the IACUC to include at least: one veterinarian (DVM/VMD), one non-scientist member, and one member unaffiliated with the research institution. Ensure all members have valid appointment documents and receive regular training.

IACUC Procedures Staffing
3

Does the veterinary care plan include preventive medicine and emergency care?

The facility must have an on-site or consulting veterinarian providing 24/7 emergency care. The preventive medicine program should include quarantine for new animals, disease surveillance, parasite control, and vaccination (where applicable). All veterinary care records must be fully documented.

Animal Welfare Veterinary Care
4

Does the animal housing environment meet species-specific requirements?

Review the space requirements in the NRC Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals. Cage area, temperature range, humidity, lighting cycle, and ventilation exchange rate for each species must be documented and regularly monitored. Environmental enrichment plans are also a key evaluation focus.

Animal Welfare Facility Management
5

Do euthanasia methods comply with AVMA standards?

AAALAC requires all euthanasia methods to follow the AVMA Guidelines for the Euthanasia of Animals. Review whether the euthanasia method used for each species is on the "acceptable" or "conditionally acceptable" list. Ensure operators are trained and have written SOPs.

Euthanasia Animal Welfare
